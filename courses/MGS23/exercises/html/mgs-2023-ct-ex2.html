Exercise 4: Isomorphisms

Show that, for a morphism f ∈C(X,Y ), there is at most one inverse. This means that, if we have two morphisms g1,g2 ∈ C(Y,X) such that g1 ∘ f = idX and f ∘ g1 = idY and g2 ∘ f = idX and f ∘ g2 = idY , then we can conclude that g1 = g2.

In other words, a morphism is an isomorphism in at most one way. (Note: Isomorphisms are defined on the previous exercise sheet.)
